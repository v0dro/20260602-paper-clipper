Privacy Policy

Paper Clipper · Last updated: 9 June 2026

Paper Clipper lets you photograph newspaper clippings, analyze them with AI, and keep them organized on your device. The app is built to be local-first and privacy-respecting: your clippings live on your device, and nothing is uploaded automatically. This policy explains exactly what data the app handles and why.

The short version: Your clippings and their AI results are stored only on your device in an encrypted database. An image leaves your device only when you choose to analyze a clipping. There are no ads, no third-party analytics, and no tracking.

What is stored on your device

  • The photos you capture of clippings.
  • AI results (transcribed text and summaries), plus any tags and comments you add.

This data is kept in an encrypted on-device database (SQLCipher on Android, with the encryption key held in the Android Keystore; SwiftData on iOS). It is not synced to any cloud service by the app.

AI analysis (the only time an image leaves your device)

When you explicitly tap to analyze a clipping, the image is sent over an encrypted (HTTPS) connection to a small proxy server operated for this app. That proxy forwards the image to Google’s Gemini API, which returns a transcription and a short summary. The result is sent back and stored on your device. No image is ever sent automatically or in the background.

  • Google processes the image under its own terms — see the Gemini API terms and the Google Privacy Policy.
  • The proxy keeps minimal access logs (timestamp, a per-install identifier, request size, app version, and outcome) used to operate the service and enforce a daily usage limit so the AI quota isn’t abused.

Identifiers

To enforce the daily analysis limit, the app sends a randomly generated per-install identifier to the proxy. If you choose to sign in (see below), your account identifier is used instead. These identifiers are not sold or shared, and are not used for advertising or cross-app tracking.

Optional sign-in

Paper Clipper may offer optional Google Sign-In. Signing in is not required to use the app, and where present it is used only to provide a stable identifier for your usage limit. The app uses no third-party analytics, advertising, or crash-tracking SDKs.

Feedback

If you send feedback from within the app, your message is delivered to the developer. Including your email address is optional — provide it only if you’d like a reply.

Export

The export feature writes your clippings (images and metadata) to a ZIP file on your own device through the system file picker. Nothing is uploaded; the file goes only where you choose to save it.

Your control over your data

  • Deleting a clipping removes it from the on-device database.
  • Clearing the app’s data or uninstalling the app permanently erases all locally stored clippings and results.
  • To remove server-side records tied to your account, use the account deletion request form.

Children

Paper Clipper is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new “last updated” date.
